# Transaction Reporting and Monitoring Rules

## Section 1: Reportable Transaction Threshold
All transactions exceeding the established threshold must be reported to relevant authorities. Thresholds are set based on transaction type and risk profile.

## Section 2: Real-Time Monitoring
Financial institutions must implement real-time transaction monitoring systems to detect suspicious patterns and anomalies.

## Section 3: Periodic Reporting
In addition to real-time alerts, comprehensive transaction reports must be submitted at defined intervals (daily, weekly, monthly).

## Section 4: Transaction Description
Each reportable transaction must include: parties involved, amounts, dates, methods, and any relevant business context.

## Section 5: Anomalous Transaction Detection
Transactions that deviate significantly from customer's normal patterns require additional review and documentation.

## Section 6: Velocity Rules
Multiple transactions in rapid succession to different beneficiaries may indicate structuring and must be flagged for review.

## Section 7: Geographic Risk Assessment
Transactions involving high-risk jurisdictions must be subject to enhanced monitoring and documentation requirements.

## Section 8: New Recipient Transactions
Payments to new or previously unverified recipients must undergo additional verification and may require higher approval levels.

## Section 9: Round-Amount Transactions
Large round-amount transactions (e.g., exact multiples of 10,000) may indicate deliberate structuring and require scrutiny.

## Section 10: Unusual Timing Patterns
Transactions occurring outside normal business hours or with unusual frequency patterns require investigation.

## Section 11: Business Context Verification
Transaction purpose must be verified against customer's declared business nature and historical patterns.

## Section 12: Reporting Timeline
Reportable transactions must be reported within defined timeframes (typically within 48-72 hours of detection).

## Section 13: Remediation Actions
When suspicious transactions are identified, appropriate remediation such as transaction blocking or customer notification may be required.

## Section 14: Compliance Audit Trail
All monitoring decisions and remediation actions must be documented with timestamps and audit trails for regulatory review.
