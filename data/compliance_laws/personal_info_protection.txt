# Personal Information Protection Laws (PIPL)

## Section 1: Personal Information Definition
Personal information refers to all kinds of information, recorded in electronic or other form, concerning an identified or identifiable individual.

## Section 2: Collection and Use Consent
Organizations must obtain prior consent before collecting personal information, except in specific circumstances such as performance of contracts or legal obligations.

## Section 3: Data Minimization
Organizations must only collect and use personal information necessary for legitimate purposes. Excessive collection is prohibited.

## Section 4: Accuracy and Timeliness
Organizations must ensure personal information is accurate and kept up to date. Inaccurate information must be corrected promptly.

## Section 5: Security Protection
Organizations must implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect personal information from unauthorized access, disclosure, or alteration.

## Section 6: Retention Limitations
Personal information must not be kept longer than necessary for its stated purposes. Retention periods must be clearly defined.

## Section 7: Cross-Border Transfers
Transfer of personal information across borders requires explicit consent and assessment of recipient country protection level.

## Section 8: Individual Rights
Individuals have rights to access, correct, delete, and port their personal information. Organizations must establish mechanisms to facilitate these rights.

## Section 9: Data Breach Notification
In case of unauthorized access or disclosure, individuals and regulators must be notified without undue delay.

## Section 10: Privacy Impact Assessment
Organizations handling large-scale personal information must conduct privacy impact assessments before deployment of new systems.

## Section 11: Automated Processing
Automated decision-making that significantly affects individuals' rights requires human review and appeal mechanisms.

## Section 12: Third-Party Processing
Processors of personal information must implement appropriate protections equivalent to those of the data controller.
